The Imperia Project

The Imperiia Project is supported by a Digital Humanities Advancement Grant from the National Endowment for the Humanities, and by the Davis Center for Russian and Eurasian Studies at Harvard University. Our project documents, interrogates, visualizes, and interprets the history of Imperial Russia. In other words, we are building a spatial history of the Russian Empire.

In other phases of the project we create databases and make maps. Here we tease out the narrative threads that run through our archive. This is where we tell stories and push the boundaries of narrative. This is where you can see the empire through a pack of playing cards. Learn the history of Kamchatka in a dozen maps. Immerse yourself in the Putrid Sea. Voyage down the Volga River on the eve of World War I. This is where we demonstrate that every shred of the historical record is part of a dense fabric of connections in which every location, every event, every thought, every...thing, has a place.

The Tsar's Trans-Atlantic Voyagers Initiative

"The Tsar's Trans-Atlantic Voyagers" is a cutting-edge research initiative of The Imperiia Project that explores the spatial and social history of migration from the Russian Empire to the United States across a significant era from 1834 to 1897. The centerpiece of this project is a comprehensive dataset provided by the US National Archives, consisting of half a million passenger arrival records and ship manifests, including historical movements over six decades. Within the scope of Geographic Information Systems (GIS), the size dataset is considerable as big data. The untouched comprehensiveness of the dataset offers provides a groundbreaking opportunity to reconstruct the social and cultural identities of those who migrated, as well as the political, economic, and geographical contexts of migration.

The project aims to unravel the complex pattens of historical migration and revolutionize history higher education by leveraging a variety of data science methodologies, complemented by machine learning algorithms and artificial intelligence capabilities. This innovative approach not only enhances the study of these patterns but also seeks to integrate these insights into academic curricula, fostering a new era of interdisciplinary collaboration. The ultimate goal of this initiative is to connect findings uncovered through extensive big data analytics with historical context, creating dynamic applications that will revolutionize both research and teaching within the field of Russian studies through the incorporation of cutting-edge technology.
